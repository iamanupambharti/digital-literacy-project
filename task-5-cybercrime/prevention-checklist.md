# Keep Yourself Safe Online: A Guide to Student Protection

*   **Turn on Multi-Factor Authentication:** For email account and college portal logins, always turn on a second layer of security, like an OTP or authenticator app.
*   **Protect the privacy of your UPI PIN by never disclosing it to third parties.** Additionally, keep in mind that you only need to enter your UPI PIN to send money—not to receive it.
*   **Scan QR Codes Carefully:** Only scan QR codes from reputable retailers' websites. Scammers may use a phony QR code to ask you to send money.
*   **Make Use of Strong, Unique Passwords:** Avoid using your birthday or registration number as a password. There should be a mix of cases, numbers, and symbols in the password.
*   **Steer clear of public Wi-Fi:** Avoid using public campus and coffee shop Wi-Fi to access your bank accounts or academic portals.
*   **Check for "HTTPS":** Before providing any information, make sure the website begins with HTTPS and that the browser's address bar has a padlock.
*   **Think Before You Click:** Be wary of messages that seem too good to be true, particularly those that offer internships via SMS or WhatsApp from senders you don't know.

## How to File a Cybercrime Report in India

If you, as a student, fall victim to an online scam, you should report it right away to the appropriate authorities. You should also report any other student who falls victim to an online scam.

*   **Portal:** https://cybercrime.gov.in/
*   **National Helpline:** 1930 (24/7)
