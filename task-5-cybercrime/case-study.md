# Case Study: Academic Phishing Attack

## What is the crime?
Phishing is a type of social engineering crime in which the hacker tricks the victim into believing he is communicating with a trusted source, when in reality he is not, and tricks him into opening a link or providing sensitive information like login details and financial information.

## How does the crime happen?
Phishing is normally done by sending a spoofed email to the victim, claiming to be from a trusted source like the University IT department or the Dean’s office. The email is normally sent in such a way that it creates a false sense of urgency, for example, claiming that the student’s academic portal account is going to be deactivated unless he "verifies" his details immediately.

Once the student opens the link, he is redirected to a spoofed website that looks exactly like the original university website. Once the student has entered his registration number and password, the hacker gets his details in real time.

## Who is normally affected?
College students are normally affected in phishing attacks because they normally receive a lot of official communication through digital channels, especially around exam time.

## Consequences
The consequences are quite serious, as the hacker can gain access to personal data, modify bank details for fee refunds, and/or spread further malware through the compromised student email to other areas of the campus network. Also, in some cases, identity theft is possible, which can cause serious damage in the future.
